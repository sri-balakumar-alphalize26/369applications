HR Field Attendance

Attendance for staff who work on the road — a whole day's journey (trips, customer visits, distance, fuel and GPS) captured on one attendance record. The full step-by-step manual is on the Open in PDF doc button.

1. Where to find it

Menu: Attendances → Field Attendance. There is also a Field Attendance app icon in the app launcher (the nine-dot grid) that opens the same screen.

  • My Field Attendance — every employee sees their own field days.
  • All Field Records — HR Officers and Managers see everyone's field attendance.
The list shows the date, employee, Check In / Check Out (in office time), the source trip, the visited stops, the location and the trip totals. Use the My Records, Late Only, With Deduction and Waived filters to narrow it down.

2. What it captures

  • A Field source flag (attendance_source = field) — field attendance is marked separately from manual office attendance.
  • A primary trip plus unlimited additional trips — a full day of stops on one record, with multi-trip support.
  • Customer visits, each linked to the trip it happened on, and an auto-derived Visited Stops display.
  • GPS latitude/longitude captured from the trip or the first visit, plus a Location name.
  • Trip Totals: Total KM Travelled, Total Duration, Total Fuel (Litres) and Total Fuel Amount — added up automatically across every trip.

3. A day in the field

The form guides you with clearly labelled buttons, in the order you press them across the day:

  • Check in (from the app or the form) — the record is marked a Field source and the guidance banner points you to the next action.
  • Enter a late reason — only if you arrived late (see section 5).
  • Setup Primary Trip (Home to Office) — pick the trip, enter the Start KM, optionally name the Location, and save; the trip starts and the primary card fills in. Going straight to a customer instead? Use Setup Secondary Trip (Home to Visit).
  • Add Additional Trip — for each new stop. If a trip is still open, a Close Previous Trip popup appears first (enter the End KM, Save & Exit); then pick the trip and visit. The Start KM is pre-filled from the previous trip's End KM, so you usually just confirm it.
  • Primary Trip (Via Office or Direct) — begin the journey home. Via Office = Visit→Office then Office→Home (the Primary Trip (Office to Home) button appears for the second leg); Direct = Visit→Home.
  • Check Out Now — ends the day. If a trip is still open you enter the End KM first; on check-out the last trip is closed, every visit is marked Done, and the whole record locks read-only.
Adding a new trip always closes the previous one first, so distance and fuel stay accurate. Ended trips are locked and hidden from the trip picker.

4. Times & trip odometer

  • Start / End Time are read-only: they are captured automatically when you start and close a trip — you no longer type them in.
  • Office time everywhere: each trip's Start/End Time and each visit's Date / Time are shown in the company's Office Timezone (the same anchor as office attendance), so they read the same for everyone.
  • Start KM carries over: the next trip's Start KM is pre-filled from the previous (last ended) trip's End KM for odometer continuity — you can still override it.

5. Late tracking is shared

Field check-ins are ordinary attendance records, so late detection, deductions and waivers work exactly like office attendance — anchored to the Office Timezone — and they appear in the Employee Report too.

If you arrived late, scroll to the Late Tracking section: the Late Reason is required before the record can be saved. Click Enter Late Reason, type why in the popup and save; once entered the button becomes Update Reason (re-opening with the existing reason), and it disappears after check-out.

6. Reviewing field attendance

Managers open All Field Records to review any employee's day. Group by Employee, Department, Trip, Date or Month, filter by My Records / Late Only / With Deduction / Waived, and open any record to inspect the trips, visits, GPS and trip totals.
