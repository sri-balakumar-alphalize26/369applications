Attendance & Late Tracking

Office hours, late detection & deductions, grace, waivers, the monthly summary and the office timezone. The full step-by-step manual is on the Open in PDF doc button.

1. Where to find it

Everything lives under Attendances → Late Tracking:

  • Late Records — every check-in with its late time, deduction and waiver status.
  • Monthly Summary — the per-employee late totals for payroll.
  • Waiver Requests (staff see My Waiver Requests) — submit, approve or reject a waiver.
  • Configuration — Office Hours & Working Days, Deduction Slabs and Public Holidays.

2. Configure the policy — Office Hours & Working Days

Menu: Configuration → Office Hours & Working Days. Each record is the policy for a company, optionally narrowed to one Department.

Working Days & Half-Day Fridays

Working days = checked weekdays (minus public holidays). A half-day Friday (a Friday in a configured position) counts as 0.5, even when Friday is unchecked.

June (Friday off, positions 2,4,5): 26 full days + 0.5 + 0.5 = 27. July (5 Fridays) → 26 + 1.5 = 27.5. Recalculated per month.

3. Deductions — Slab vs Hourly

Both apply only after the grace quota is used up and are 0 if waived.

Fixed (Slab-based)

Menu: Configuration → Deduction Slabs. The late minutes match a slab row (Late From ≤ late ≤ Late To; Late To = 0 means “and above”) and that slab's flat amount is deducted.

Hourly Wage

hourly_rate = monthly wage / working days in month / daily work hours deduction = hourly_rate × ceil(late_minutes / 60) (every started hour = 1 hour)
No-wage fallback: in Hourly mode, if an employee has no wage the hourly amount is 0, so it falls back to the matching slab. With no slab, the deduction is 0. Keep a basic slab as a safety net.

Public Holidays

Menu: Configuration → Public Holidays — add each holiday with a Name and Date. Holidays are removed from the working-days count, so no one is marked late on a day off.

4. How late detection works

  • The UTC check-in is converted to the Office Timezone.
  • The session is determined; expected start = the session start (or the half-day-Friday start).
  • If the local check-in is later than start + Late Threshold, it is Is Late, with Late Time measured from the start time.
Example (Asia/Muscat): check-in stored 04:10 UTC = 08:10 Muscat; start 08:00 → 10 min late.

5. Late Records, Reasons & Grace

Menu: Late Tracking → Late Records. Each row shows Is Late, Late Time (H:MM), Session, Half Day, the Late # in the month, the Deduction Amount and whether it was Waived. The Late Reason now appears as a column by default, and the Late Only, With Deduction, Missing Reason and group-by filters help you focus.

  • Grace: the first N late occurrences per month (per session) are free; a reason is still required.
  • Late Reason is mandatory: a late attendance cannot be saved without a Late Reason. Saving a late record with an empty reason shows a popup asking you to enter it first. (On-time records save normally; a waived record needs no reason.)
  • Enter / Update Reason button: on a late record click Enter Late Reason; before you save, the popup previews the late time, the session, the expected start, the Late # in the month and the Deduction — so you see exactly what will be charged — then type the reason and save (this fills the reason without leaving the form). Once a reason exists the button becomes Update Reason and re-opens with the existing wording for editing. The button is hidden once a reason is filled, when the record is waived, and after check-out (the record is then read-only).

7. Monthly Late Summary

Menu: Late Tracking → Monthly Summary. Choose the Month and Year, optionally a Department or specific Employees, then Generate Summary. Each row shows Total Late Days, Total Late Time (H:MM) and Total Deductions.

It never over-deducts: the summary counts only the late occurrences after the monthly grace quota and never counts waived records, so the figures match what payroll should deduct.

8. Office-time display

Standard Odoo date columns show the viewer's timezone. The attendance lists/kanban/form, Late Waiver, Employee Report and the Late Attendance PDF show dedicated Office-Time values anchored to the Office Timezone, so everyone sees the same office time regardless of their settings. The same Office-Time display also covers Vehicle Tracking (trip Start/End Time) and Customer Visits (Date and Time) — see the HR Field Attendance guide.
